01 — ABOUT ME

A bit about who I am

I'm Kenneth Solomon, a Computer Science undergraduate at St. Wilfred's College of Computer Science, Mumbai, affiliated with the University of Mumbai. My specialisation is cybersecurity, with a strong focus on offensive techniques and understanding how systems fail under real-world attack conditions.

I practise consistently on HackTheBox — working through Active Directory attack chains, privilege escalation paths, lateral movement scenarios, and network exploitation labs. Each machine I root gets fully documented with methodology notes.

In early 2026, I presented peer-reviewed research at IRCBDAV 2026 on machine-learning-based detection of OWASP web application attacks. My Random Forest classifier outperformed SVM, KNN, Decision Tree, and Logistic Regression on the CSIC 2010 HTTP dataset.

"I don't just study security — I practise it, build with it, and document every step."

Alongside security, I've built practical tools: a network vulnerability scanner and a multi-language C++ code editor. My certification path is deliberate — HTB → CPTS → OSCP → OSEP → CRTO — building deep before going wide.
